El Diario de Juárez

UI design + news website + re-design

This is one of the biggest projects I worked on earlier in my career. I was employed at a local newspaper as the only graphic designer in the web team, meaning I designed everything to do with the digital edition at www.diario.mx. Our team consisted of a webmaster, two developers and one designer. We were assigned to propose a redesign concept of the whole website.

Before initiating, we discussed the main goals and what we wanted to convey in our proposal:

  • * Show information as clearly as possible.
  • * Get visitors to stay longer.

Aside from this, higher ups insisted on two requirements:

  • * To show as many headlines as possible
  • * Avoid color in the actual UI.

My main focus was structuring the main page and showing every article in an organized manner. Each section needed to comply with showing the most information possible. I had more freedom in some special sections such as multimedia and photo galleries.

The design phase was completed and was ready to go into development when a different approach was taken.
